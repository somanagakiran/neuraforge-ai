How Quantum Navigation Works

Quantum Navigation Systems use cold atom interferometry to measure acceleration and rotation with extreme precision.

  • Cold Atom Sensors: Atoms cooled to near absolute zero
  • Laser Interferometry: Measures atomic wave interference patterns
  • Quantum Superposition: Atoms exist in multiple states simultaneously
  • Entanglement: Particles linked across space for precise correlation
  • Dead Reckoning: Calculates position from initial known point

The system measures changes in the quantum states of atoms to detect even the slightest movements.
